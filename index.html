A Quiet Space

Not to persuade only to be honest, and to show steady effort.

I want to say this the way I should’ve said it from the start:
I was wrong.

When I was getting close to you,
I was still in a relationship I should’ve ended earlier.
I told myself I was already leaving…
but the truth is, it wasn’t over yet.
And that made what I did unfair to her, and especially to you.

You didn’t deserve to be pulled into something unfinished.
You didn’t deserve uncertainty.
And you didn’t deserve to question whether I might hurt you someday the same way.

Your fear makes sense.
Your disappointment makes sense.
Your distance makes sense.

I’m not here to explain it away.
I’m here to acknowledge exactly what I failed to do:
to be honest, clean, and fully free before choosing someone new.

I’m not asking you to trust me right now.
I’m not asking for forgiveness,
and I’m not asking for a relationship.

I just want to take responsibility
clearly, quietly, and without pressure.

Thank you for giving this a moment.
Even reading this is more grace than I deserve.

Thank you for giving this a moment.

Privacy: This page does not record or send your input. Everything here is symbolic and private to your screen.

I see you. I won’t hide from your questions.
If and when you ask, I will answer honestly outside this site directly, without defense.

What I’m Changing

I’m not promising miracles. I’m practicing real change mahall:

  • I pause before reacting so I don’t hurt you with impulses.
  • I journal to understand why I chose dishonesty instead of courage.
  • I answer hard questions directly, even when the truth is uncomfortable.
  • I give space without taking it personally.
  • I show care through steady, repeated actions not just words.

Whatever you choose silence, distance, or even a small step closer I will respect it fully.

You are not obligated to fix this.
You are not responsible for my remorse.
Your peace matters more than my hopes.

If forgiveness ever comes, it should come because you healed — not because I asked for it.
For now, I want you to know:

You’re safe here.
You’re respected here.
Your choice will always be yours.

Thank you for giving this a moment.
